The gross domestic product (GDP)

measures of national income and output for a given country's economy. The gross domestic product (GDP) is equal to the total expenditures for all final goods and services produced within the country in a stipulated period of time

Government Revenues

refer to all receipts the government gets, including taxes, custom duties, revenue from state-owned enterprises, capital revenues and foreign aid. Government Revenues are part of government budget balance calculation.

The GDP per capita

is obtained by dividing the country’s gross domestic product, adjusted by inflation, by the total population.
